The Dark Web

Complete privacy is almost impossible to have nowadays, but it is important to have a certain amount of freedom. This is not as big a problem in the U.S. as it is in other countries, but another country where the Internet is censored. Access is blocked to certain websites because of controversial information contained within. Who decides what information should be blocked? Could this lead to other websites being blocked simply because the one in charge does not agree a certain point of view?

I like your point about WiFi access points. Many people believe that these are secure, but it would not be difficult to set up a hotspot named "Free Wifi", then snoop through the traffic. Even USB charging stations can be used to gather data from unsuspecting people ("juice jacking"), though this has not become a prevalent problem yet.

Privacy is necessary for peace and in order to not become insane. Even if we don't have things to hide, that doesn't mean we want others to be able to access them easily or without our permission. If you lose access to your privacy, others can use your information to their own means, which may not be in your best interest. This reminds me of a comic I enjoy reading, and this particular one talks about the importance of having control over our information.
