Understanding Your Market
Choosing a Product to Sell
Think about business model before thinking of software
Pick an area of interest
Physical vs. Virtual products
Product acquisition, storage, fulfillment
Create the goods yourself
Cheap, but time-sensitive
Wholesale/resale (large amount bought, markup)
Need capital for initial order
Drop-Shipping: Take orders, wholesaler sends product to customer
Marketing needed to separate store from competition
Manufacture product for direct sale
Make sure product will sell before ordering units
Virtual: Take time to create, but can be sold multiple times easily
Affiliate marketing, bring traffic to your site
Determine who you will market to!
Defining Your Audience
Look at it from customer’s point of view
Create personas, representation of customers
Beginners vs. experienced users, cater differently
Focus on price vs. focus on experience
“If you chase two rabbits, you will not catch either one”
Choose WordPress theme to resonate with audience
Organizing Your Products
Sell individually, bundled, as add-ons, customizable, subscription
Gravity Forms vs. Ninja Forms
Gravity Forms = one fee, access everything, fixed cost
Ninja Forms = free, but charge for advanced functionality
Both successful, different clientele
Bundles = tells users what to expect
Beginner bundle = makes users confident as right choice
Give price break as incentives or add products as add-ons
E.g. If sell photographs, frames as add-ons
Configurable items like computers = make sure platform can handle
Subscriptions = make it easy to get your product, convenience
Offering Pre-Sales to Validate Your Product
People saying they’ll buy differs from actually buying
Try to sell just one product
Kickstarter = newer avenue for testing waters
Crowdfunding = gauge interest and get pre-orders
Get feedback as to why people didn’t pre-order
Incorporate feedback into your pre-order page
Have option to cancel pre-orders if needed
Social proof = important to build trust between you and people
Get validation = more sure footing that your time will pay off
Marketing Your Site
Using Marketing to Increase Site Traffic
Getting people to your store is difficult
Need to invest either Time or Money, or both
Easiest way = advertising
Google Adwords and Facebook Advertising = biggest players
Ads very effective, but require money to get started
Optimize your search engine ranking (SEO)
Content Marketing = create valuable content on your site
Once content is written, can continuously bring people to your site
Social media = great for sharing and creating relationships
Frame your message to the medium
Cost of Acquisition = effort and money invested in getting users
Understanding Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
Cost of Acquisition = CoA
Customer Lifetime Value = CLV
Profitable when CLV > CoA
CoA = cost per visit / conversion rate
$1.00/click / 0.20 conversion = $5.00 CoA
Incentivize buying more, variety packs!
Newsletters to remind customers to return
Related products, etc.
Increasing CLV with Repeat Customers
Encourage people to come back
Create high-quality product = smaller CoA
However, cost of R&D, branding, development higher
Advantage in regularly consumed products
Subscriptions = harder to get, but more profitable
Encourage customers to follow = maintaining contact
Don’t just promote sales all the time, be useful
Newsletters helpful for seasonal businesses
Autoresponders = target customer for related items
Email if hasn’t come back in awhile
Increasing Your Average Order Value
Premium version of products = higher quality goods
More expensive material or finish
Sell add-on that adds value to original purchase
Price anchoring: Start with high number, other items seem reasonable
One high-priced item makes others look more affordable
Upsell = similar but more expensive shown on same page
Cross-sell = promote a related item
Offer free shipping when a certain threshold exceeded
Tiered pricing = offer discounts for larger quantities
If only increase order value by 4%, still large amount in long run
Selling Products
Getting Paid
Website, Payment Gateway, Merchant Account, Bank = get paid
Website sends information to payment gateway
Payment gateway checks whether funds available
Traditional Gateway = merchant account draws funds -> bank
Modern gateway = combines payment gateway + merchant account
Brick & Mortar stores still need merchant accounts
Credit cards require more security
Authorize = verify you can make charge
Capturing = actually transfer the funds
Only supposed to capture charge when you ship the item
Examining Your Needs
What type of payment do you want to accept?
PayPal and CC, different customers different preferences
Credit Cards, PayPal, Amazon, etc.
Foreign currency = exchange fees
Stripe = robust API, many options
How much risk willing to accept?
Onsite gateway = riskier than off-site gateway
Checkout broken into two parts, billing + shipping
Then redirected to enter CC info, then redirected back
Site never sees CC info = more secure
See supported options, recurring payments, modifying amounts
Checking out with PayPal
Options: PayPal Standard, Express, Advanced
WooCommerce = WordPress eCommerce Plugin
Full options of a cart
Checkout = PayPal selected
Redirected to PayPal, back to store, store notified of payment
Many eCommerce plugins offer PayPal Standard free
Checking out with Stripe
Stripe can get direct payment from customer using CC
No restrictions on eCommerce functionality
Stripe has a test mode, processes and shows success
Store confirms that transaction authorized -> user sees order received
Backend = new order for store
SSL Certificate needed when using Credit Cards!
Shipping, Orders, Taxes
Calculating Shipping Costs
Costs and options vary, even with digital distribution
Flat Rate shipping = per item cost, easy setup
Free shipping = popular with customers
Free shipping at price point = spend more to avoid paying shipping
Let customers know prominently
Estimates = get rates directly from shipping provider
Record weight and dimensions of every single item and box
Pass quote to user, calculate estimated costs
Pay attention to personas, give customers what they want
Managing Orders
Need to ship, track, refund, and complete orders
Explore advanced features like manual order creation
Special log in for shop manager
WooCommerce = takes care of order details
Extensions allow you to customize, such as print labels from home
Inventory error, need to make sure you can address them
Partial refunds possible?
Search orders by ID or reference number
Read up on refund process for your platform
Managing Inventory and Listings across Platforms
Sync between systems
Amazon = not your customers, no access to their data
However, Amazon has great reach and simple to set up
Services like Trade Gecko automate management of inventory
Webgility = more accounting focused
QuickBooks integration, SihpStation = all-around
Know that many solutions exist for your situation
Understanding Taxes
Some countries = federal tax, state tax, county tax, city tax
Get information from a TAX PROFESSIONAL!
If you have a nexus in a place, need to pay those taxes
Nexus = physical presence, like warehouse/storefront
CO store, warehouse in UT, need to collect in both
Depends on where customer is, usually only collect once
EU = tax-inclusive prices
US = tax-exclusive prices
TaxCloud = service for gathering tax rates for you
TaxJar = prepares sales tax
eCommerce platforms should support obtaining location for taxes
Certain products may be tax-exempt
Some food, clothing, medicine, or medical equipment are exempt
Before starting, reach out to a tax professional!
Collecting EU Value-Added Tax (VAT)
2015, must pay country’s taxes if customer in EU
WooThemes.com = automatically detects location and adjusts prices
Country can be manually selected
If IP doesn’t match country, ask for manual confirmation
WooCommerce + Easy Digital Downloads => Taxamo integration
Gumroad handles new EU VAT laws well
Detect location, confirm if necessary, collect taxes, and files for you
Securing Your Site
Understanding the Basics of Secure Sockets Layer (SSL)
Security is important for three big reasons
Can be held liable for stolen credit cards
Can be fined if there is a breach
Lose customers’ trust if there is a breach
Secure-looking site increases conversions
Secure site = information encrypted, uses HTTPS
Use HTTPS requires SSL certificate
Even phone numbers should be secured for customers
WP Engine = allows you to purchase SSL through them
Installing SSL Certificate = technical, takes a few hours to sort
PCI compliance = industry standard on handling CC numbers
Most platforms for eCommerce neither PCI compliant nor violating
Woothemes.com has a guide for PCI compliance
WooCommerce can only handle 3 out of 12 points required
Rest is up to organization + host
Gumroad/Shopify take care of compliance
PCI compliance demanding, easier to start with an off-site gateway
Protecting Sensitive Data
Need to force users to use SSL, prevent regular HTTP traffic
Open up .htaccess file and make adjustments
OR let eCommerce platform handle it for you
“Force secure checkout” = good option
HTTPS SSL redirection = plugin to force all pages in HTTPS
Don’t want to secure admin login? Use third-party system
Options = Jetpack’s Single Sign on Feature or Clef
Clef = launches app on phone for verification
Let you log in without worrying about password being discovered
Lock EVERYTHING down before launch
Even one insecure transaction could be devastating

Implementing Third-Party Platforms
Hosted platforms less flexible but require little oversight

No way to rearrange page elements :(

Shopify stable, but lacks customizability
